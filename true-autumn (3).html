The simple rule: If it looks icy, pastel, or neon — skip it. If it looks rich, earthy, warm, and grounded — you're safe. Think: "Warm first. Rich second."

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere. Clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

Your palette is limitless. The True Autumn palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches in this guide are carefully chosen examples and reference points to help you understand the qualities to look for, warm, earthy, and richly grounded. When you are shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display. On other Android devices it may be called Night Light or Blue Light Filter under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] warm or cool toned? Does it suit True Autumn?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point. It has limitations and pulls from what is available online, so always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

Accessories & Eyewear

The bigger picture: Every colour in your palette can be referenced for accessories. A warm rust bag, a camel scarf, a deep garnet nail — it all works. The guidance below helps you understand why certain choices feel more harmonious, so you can shop with confidence rather than follow a checklist.

Warm amber, brown, or bronze lenses are your most harmonious options. Olive-green tinted lenses also work beautifully. Avoid cool grey, blue-grey, or mirrored silver lenses, they cast a cool light that works against your warm colouring. A warm copper or amber mirror lens is a great statement choice for True Autumn.

Scarves, Hats & Wraps
Near-face accessories matter most
Anything worn close to your face, scarves, hat brims, collars, has the same impact as a top or blouse. Use your Near The Face colours as a guide here. A camel scarf, a rust hat, or a forest green wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a warm-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit into something that truly works for your season.

For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories

Warm brown, olive, and camel suits are your strongest base. Gold or bronze cufflinks over silver. A warm cream or ivory shirt over stark white. An olive or burgundy tie over grey or black.
Makeup & Hair
Your True Autumn guide to beauty. Warm, earthy, and naturally radiant. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Product recommendations are included as helpful starting points. Formulas and shade names can change — always do a quick check before purchasing, and trust your eye against your palette above all else.
Your golden rule: If it looks cool, icy, or blue-toned — put it back. True Autumn makeup is warm, earthy, and naturally glowing. A warm terracotta lip with minimal eye makeup will always look more "you" than a full cool face. Your colours create glow without needing heavy makeup, that's the magic of wearing your season.
Lips

Your "nude" lip, warm, not cool beige
Cool nudes wash you out. Your version of a "no-makeup lip" is a warm nude, soft peach, or sheer caramel balm. Look for shades described as warm nude, warm beige, or caramel. Try: ILIA Balmy Tint in a warm peachy tone, RMS Lip2Cheek in Smile worn lightly, or a warm brown lip liner worn alone.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: warm or neutral-warm undertone only. Golden or peachy-based foundations work best. Pink-based or cool foundations will look ashy on you.
What to look for
Ask for golden, warm, or peachy undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as pink, cool, or neutral-cool. For tinted moisturisers, ILIA Skin Tint in warmer shades and RMS Beauty "Un" Cover-Up in golden tones are great natural options. Tip: when testing foundation on your wrist, a match that looks warm and alive is right. One that looks ashy or pink means it is too cool for you.
Eyes
Your eyes thrive with warm definition and earthy depth. Bronze and copper metallic finishes catch the light beautifully on you. Reach for the warm brown palette, it was made for you.
Eyeshadow, what works
Base shades: warm ivory, golden beige, soft peach, light caramel. Depth: warm brown, chocolate, dark olive, burnt sienna, deep teal. Accents: bronze, copper, warm gold, moss green, spiced plum. Satin and warm metallic finishes are your friend. Avoid: cool grey, silver, icy lavender, black, or any cool metallic.
Eyeliner
Warm brown is your most flattering liner. If you want more definition, try dark chocolate, warm espresso, or deep olive. Avoid black or cool grey liner, which creates a harsh contrast that fights your natural warmth. Tip: a warm brown liner with a terracotta lip is a quintessential True Autumn look.
Mascara
Warm brown mascara is your most flattering choice. Dark brown or espresso mascara works beautifully too. Black mascara can work but may feel harsh, if you use it, pair with softer eye makeup elsewhere. Try: Physician Formula Butter Mascara in Brown, or any brown-black shade for a softer, more natural finish.
Brows & Beard
Brow guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade warmer. If you have dark hair, a warm dark brown brow product works beautifully. If your hair is lighter, opt for a warm taupe or golden brown, never an ash or cool grey-brown. Tip: ask for "warm" or "golden brown" tones when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Beard & grooming colour
If you colour your beard or brows, look for warm brown shades, chestnut, warm brown, or golden brown are excellent. Avoid anything with an ash, cool, or blue-black tone. When in doubt, stay warm rather than going darker, and always do a strand test first. Tip: warm-toned beards look excellent on True Autumn colouring, lean into it.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. True Autumn's natural colouring tends toward warm, rich, medium-to-deep hair, and that is exactly what flatters you most.
Your best hair colours
Rich and warm: warm brown, chestnut, golden dark blonde, auburn, rich copper tones. Depth options: warm espresso or dark chocolate brown, look for shades with red or golden undertones. Avoid: ash brown, blue-black, light blonde, platinum, or icy highlights, these pull cool and create tension with your natural warm glow.
Highlights & colour techniques
If you highlight, choose warm golden, caramel, or honey tones. Balayage in warm amber or copper is stunning on True Autumn. Avoid: cool ash highlights, platinum balayage, or any technique that introduces icy or silver tones. Warmth is the goal, it looks natural and alive on you.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "warm toned," "golden," or "no ash" for your shade. Show your colourist your palette, having your actual warm colour reference makes the conversation so much easier and avoids misunderstandings. Tip: when in doubt, go warmer and richer rather than cooler or lighter.
